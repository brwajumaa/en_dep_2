Morophology

Course Description

Course Code: LIT12305

/

Credit Hours: 3

/

Prerequisite Course: LIT11300

/

Co-requisite Course: LIT2300


This course is designed to help learners who study English language to be familiar with an important area of language which is morphology. As part of the requirement, when a language learner studies a language, they have to study knowledge of the language as well. Morphology, as the study of the process of word-formation, is an important part of studying any language. This course starts with basic definition of a morpheme and ends with covering the process of word formation. Types of morphemes, their syntactic and semantic functions, the process of affixation, typ es of affixes and the process of word formation are among the topics to be covered in this course.
